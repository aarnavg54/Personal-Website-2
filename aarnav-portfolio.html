About Me

Passionate about using AI to solve real-world problems

I'm a sophomore at Vernon Hills High School with a deep passion for computer science, artificial intelligence, and mathematics. My journey in technology began with a curiosity about how machines can learn and adapt, which has evolved into active research in deep learning and medical imaging.

Currently, I'm conducting cutting-edge research in two exciting areas: pulmonary blood vessel segmentation using U-Net deep learning architectures under Dr. Viktoriia Liu at the Aspiring Scholars Directed Research Program (ASDRP), and analyzing storefront neuromarketing techniques using large language models at SiriusMindShare Lab with Dr. Venkata Duvvuri.

Beyond research, I'm actively involved in competitive mathematics and science competitions, having achieved state-level recognition in Science Olympiad and Math Team competitions. I believe in the power of continuous learning and regularly pursue advanced coursework outside of school through platforms like IBM Machine Learning, Neural Networks and Deep Learning, and MIT's Beaver Works Summer Institute.

My goal is to contribute meaningfully to the scientific community by developing AI solutions that can positively impact healthcare, business intelligence, and society at large. I'm always excited to collaborate on innovative projects and connect with fellow researchers and enthusiasts.
